## What Went Well

- **Rapid Integration:**  
  The initial setup of the frontend application made it significantly easier to connect backend profiles. The existing frontend foundation enabled a fast and smooth integration with Supabase services.

- **Template-Driven Development:**  
  Using Figma-based template code provided by the UI/UX Lead allowed the frontend team to move from design to implementation with minimal boilerplate and friction.

- **PR Efficiency & Review Culture:**  
  The team established a high-velocity Pull Request cycle, with quick review turnarounds. Deployment previews enabled immediate validation of UI updates and bug fixes.

- **Agile Resilience**  
  Despite being the first sprint with a new tech stack, the team successfully completed **50% of committed story points** and achieved **60–80% completion** on remaining stories.

- **Constant Communication:**  
  The team maintained strong responsiveness across all channels, followed the defined project structure, and effectively used logs to troubleshoot merging issues.

---

##  What Could Be Improved

- **Technical Roadblocks:**  
  Challenges with **route protection** (e.g., preventing direct URL access to restricted pages like matches) took more time than initially estimated.

- **Late-Stage Scope Creep**  
  Unplanned feature additions (such as image validation) introduced late in the sprint created unnecessary pressure on backend development.

- **Testing Alignment Confusion**  
  Misalignment between User Story requirements and automated tests caused confusion, especially when criteria were introduced mid-sprint.

- **Task Granularity & "Crunch"**  
  Tasks were too broadly defined, leading to a **time crunch** for UI/UX and Frontend teams toward the end of the sprint.

- **AC Verbiage**  
  Acceptance Criteria were sometimes too vague, making it difficult for the Product Owner to determine clear **pass/fail outcomes** during validation.

---

## Action Items for Next Sprint

- **More Strict Scope Guardrails**  
  Enforce a **"No New Requirements"** rule during the sprint. Any new requests (e.g., image validation) will be added to the Product Backlog for future prioritization.

- **Synchronized Test Planning**  
  Developers and testers will conduct a **pre-development sync** to ensure test cases align directly with Acceptance Criteria.

- **Task Granularization**  
  Break down UI and Frontend stories into **smaller, manageable tasks** that can be completed quicker to improve workflow and reduce end-of-sprint pressure.

- **AC Verbiage Audit**  
  Refine all Sprint 2 User Stories to ensure Acceptance Criteria are **clear, measurable, and binary** for easier validation.

- **Story Point Calibration**  
  Reassess and adjust story point estimates based on lessons learned from **session management** and **route protection** complexities in Sprint 1.
