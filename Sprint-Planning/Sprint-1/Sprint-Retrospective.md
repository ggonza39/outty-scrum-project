## What Went Well

- **Technical Infrastructure:** The integration of Supabase as a backend-as-a-service proved to be highly practical, allowing for rapid database and authentication setup.

- **Frontend Acceleration:** The hand-off process was streamlined by the UI/UX lead providing functional template code, which allowed the Frontend team to bypass boilerplate setup and move straight to feature logic.

- **Agile Resilience:** The team demonstrated high adaptability in bug-squashing and problem-solving, ensuring that completed User Stories met the full Definition of Done (DoD) despite mid-sprint technical hurdles.

- **Deployment & Flow:** The CI/CD pipeline, PR system, and project structure remained stable and intuitive. Communication across all roles was frequent and transparent.
  
---

## What Could Be Improved

- **Late-Stage Scope Creep:** There were instances of "feature creep" (e.g., requests for image validation) that were not defined in the initial Sprint 1 scope, creating unnecessary pressure on the Backend.

- **Testing Alignment:** Discrepancies arose between the User Story requirements and the automated test suite. Testing criteria were occasionally introduced that hadn't been discussed during Sprint Planning, leading to developer confusion.

- **Task Granularity & Time Management:** The UI/UX and Frontend teams experienced a significant "time crunch." Current task structures are too broad, making it difficult to balance design iterations with development deadlines.

- **Acceptance Criteria (AC) Clarity:** Some AC verbiage was deemed too vague, making it difficult for the Product Owner and Testers to objectively verify if a story was "Done."

---

## Action Items for Next Sprint
- **More Strict Scope Guardrails:** No new requirements (like image validation) will be added mid-sprint. Out-of-scope requests will be moved to the Product Backlog for Sprint 2/3 evaluation.

- **Synchronized Test Planning:** The Tester and Developers will review User Stories together before coding begins to ensure the test suite aligns 1:1 with the stated Acceptance Criteria.

- **Task Streamlining:** Implement a more granular task breakdown for UI/UX and Frontend roles in Sprint 2 to provide a clearer daily roadmap and reduce "crunch" anxiety.

- **AC Refinement:** All User Stories for Sprint 2 will undergo a "Verbiage Audit" to ensure criteria are tangible, measurable, and binary (Yes/No) for easier testing.

- **Story Point Calibration:** Recalibrate for Sprint 2 due to partial completion of User Stories of Sprint 1.
