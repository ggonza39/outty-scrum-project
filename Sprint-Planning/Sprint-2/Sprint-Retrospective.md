## What Went Well

- **Strong Team Communication:**  
  Communication significantly improved during Sprint 2. Team members maintained consistent responsiveness, quickly addressed issues, and supported each other across frontend, backend, and UI/UX tasks.

- **Team Flexibility & Resource Sharing:**  
  The team demonstrated strong flexibility by jumping in where help was needed. Members dynamically supported different areas of development, allowing work to continue smoothly even when unexpected issues arose.

- **Improved Team Coordination:**  
  Team members showed a better understanding of each other's strengths and responsibilities. This improved coordination allowed smoother execution of tasks and contributed to Sprint 2 being viewed as the team’s strongest sprint so far.

- **Fast Issue Resolution:**  
  When gaps or missing details in user stories were identified, they were quickly communicated and resolved. Rapid responsiveness helped prevent small issues from becoming larger blockers.

- **Overall Sprint Performance:**  
  The sprint was widely regarded by team members as the most successful sprint to date. The team demonstrated consistent execution across all functional areas, including frontend, backend, UI/UX, and issue tracking.

---

## What Could Be Improved

- **User Story Clarity & Acceptance Criteria Detail:**  
  Some user stories lacked specific implementation details, leading to confusion about expected outcomes (such as profile image persistence behavior). More detailed Acceptance Criteria would reduce ambiguity and improve alignment across developers.

- **Missing Feature Details in User Stories:**  
  A few functional requirements were unintentionally omitted from user stories, causing minor confusion during implementation. These gaps created uncertainty about whether certain features belonged to the current sprint.

- **Secondary Review of User Stories:**  
  Because user story preparation relies heavily on a single contributor, small items occasionally slipped through the cracks. Introducing a secondary review process could help catch missing details early.

- **Burndown Chart Timing & Work Visibility:**  
  Delayed commits created the appearance of late progress on the burndown chart, even when work was already completed.

---

## Action Items for Next Sprint

- **Enhance Acceptance Criteria Detail**  
  Ensure all user stories include clear and specific Acceptance Criteria so developers fully understand expected outcomes before beginning implementation.

- **Introduce Secondary User Story Review**  
  Add a second reviewer to validate user stories before sprint start to reduce the likelihood of missing functional requirements.

- **Encourage Earlier Code Commits**  
  Push completed work in smaller increments throughout the sprint instead of waiting until the end. This will improve visibility on the burndown chart and better reflect actual progress.

- **Continue Early Gap Reporting**  
  Team members should continue reporting missing details or unclear requirements as soon as they are identified so adjustments can be made quickly.

- **Maintain Current Communication Practices**  
  Continue leveraging strong communication habits that contributed to Sprint 2 success and team efficiency.
